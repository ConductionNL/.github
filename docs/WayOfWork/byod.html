BYOD — Bring Your Own Device

Conduction operates a Bring Your Own Device model: you use your own laptop and your own mobile phone for work — there is no company-issued hardware. This page describes the requirements, plus the rules around two-factor authentication and reporting your devices.

Approved by the management team (April 2026).

Your laptop / development machine​

Pick what works for you. Requirements:

  1. It can run the required local tooling.
  2. It meets the security baseline — see Security policy for antivirus, encryption, and VPN.

Your mobile phone​

Conduction lets you use your personal mobile for work, with one important rule about email.

Rule: Gmail only via the Gmail app​

Access to your Conduction Gmail on mobile is only allowed via the official Gmail app (Android or iOS). It is not allowed to add your Conduction account to:

  • the native Mail app on iOS
  • Samsung Mail, Outlook, Spark, or other third-party mail clients
  • IMAP/POP connections on desktop clients via your phone

Why​

When your account lives in the Gmail app, it remains manageable from the Conduction Workspace domain. On offboarding (or in case of device loss/theft), access can be revoked centrally without physical access to the device: the account is detached, and any synced mail/data on the device immediately becomes inaccessible.

When native or third-party clients are used, a local copy of the mail is created outside the reach of central management — it persists after offboarding and is an audit risk (ISO 27001 A.8 / A.9, access control and asset management).

Two-factor authentication (2FA)​

Purpose: protect company data and accounts against misuse and data breaches.

Logging into a work account requires two factors:

  1. Your password
  2. An additional code from an authenticator app

Who does this apply to?​

Every employee with access to email, company systems, cloud environments, customer data, or administrative data. In practice: everyone.

Approved authenticator per service​

Which authenticator you use depends on the system — a 2FA app only authenticates the services it is built to support.

SystemAuthenticator
Google Workspace (Gmail, Drive, Calendar)Google Authenticator (or Google Prompt)
ESET-protected services (VPN, ESET console)ESET Authenticator (= ESET Secure Authentication)
Other work systemsFollow what that system requires. When in doubt, ask IT.

Other general authenticator apps (Microsoft Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, etc.) are not allowed for work accounts — for each service, use the app listed above.

Mandatory rules​

  • Two-factor authentication is mandatory for all work accounts.
  • You may not disable this protection.
  • Only approved verification methods may be used (see above).
  • No 2FA = no access. Accounts without active 2FA will be revoked.

Protecting your codes​

  • Never share codes with anyone — not with colleagues, not with team leads.
  • Store backup (recovery) codes in a safe place (e.g. your password manager). Do not print them unencrypted and do not store them in your email.
  • Lost phone or lost access to your authenticator: see Lost or stolen device.

Reporting devices​

Any device you use for Conduction work must be known to your team lead — required for access control and asset management (ISO 27001).

New device (mobile or laptop)​

  1. Report the device to your team lead before you start using it for work.
  2. Mobile:
    • Install the Gmail app and add your @conduction.nl account.
    • Install Google Authenticator and link your Google Workspace account for 2FA.
    • Install ESET Authenticator for ESET-protected services (ask IT during setup).
  3. Laptop: make sure you meet the Security policy requirements (antivirus, encryption, VPN).

Lost or stolen device​

Report it immediately to your team lead. They will arrange the revoke with IT: your work account is detached from Workspace and the 2FA link is revoked. You don't need to wipe anything on the device yourself.

This also applies if you lose just your authenticator (e.g. a new phone without migration): report it so you can be re-enrolled.

Removing company data on offboarding​

When you leave Conduction, you are responsible for removing all company data from your personal devices. For your mobile, central detachment handles it (see above). For your laptop, follow the standard offboarding process — see Onboarding & Offboarding.
